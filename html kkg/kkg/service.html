MATERIAL OPTIONS

Your product, and the look you would like to achieve, have a big impact on the materials used in your packaging.

We offer all the standard options, such as various types of plastic, acrylic, glass, aluminum, and bamboo, and we’re happy to source alternative materials to create your ideal packaging product.

Not sure what materials to use? Our engineering experts can help advise you here.

PP

Polypropylene

PP (Polypropylene) is a thermoplastic polymer used in a wide variety of applications.

PET

Polypropylene

PET (Polyethylene terephthalate) is commonly recycled. Depending on its processing and thermal history, PET may exist both as a transparent and as a semi crystalline polymer. The semi crystalline material might appear transparent or opaque depending on its crystal structure and particle size.

PETG

Polypropylene

PETG (Polyethylene terephthalate glycol) is a clear amorphous thermoplastic that can be injection molded or sheet extruded. It can be colored during processing.

PE

Polypropylene

PE (Polyethylene) is the most common plastic and is available in LDPE (low density), MDPE (medium density) and HDPE (hi-density).

Acrylic

Polypropylene

PMMA (Polymethyl methacrylate), also known as acrylic is commonly used in the beauty and cosmetic industries.

GLASS

Glass is a non-crystalline amorphous solid that is often transparent and has widespread uses.

ALUMINUM

Aluminum is 100% recyclable without any loss of its natural qualities.

BAMBOO

Bamboo is a natural and organic material for an environmentally-friendly packaging solution. It can be made into different shapes according to your needs and can even be personalized with an engraving.

FINISHING OPTIONS

Another important factor in achieving your desired look is in the way your packaging is finished.

We offer many different options for you to choose from, including in-mold color, inner and outer sprays, metallization, embossing and debossing, and spray finishes like pearl, matte, soft touch, glossy, and frosted

Silk screen

Silk screening is the process in which ink is pressed through a photographically treated screen onto the surface to be decorated. One color is applied at a time with a new screen for each color.

AVAILABLE ON:

Aluminum, Acrylic, HDPE, LDPE, PET

PRINTING OPTIONS

Lastly, the final piece of the puzzle that is your unique look is how your branding and product information are printed onto your packaging.

Here, we offer a full range of options, including silk screening, offset printing, water and heat transferring, hot and foil stamping, U/V coating, and laser etching.

If you feel overwhelmed by the options, our experts can help guide you. Contact us
